Watershed ecosystem science has identified plenty of idiosyncrasy within watersheds, but hasn't produced many general theories about watersheds at large. A major reason has been the challenge of data access and integration across all the organizations that house watershed data. (LTER, CZO/CZ Net, NEON, DOE, USFS, etc.). MacroSheds unites stream and watershed data from all these sources on one platform, making it easy for anyone to explore the hydrology, water quality, and biogeochemistry of rivers across North America and beyond.

This is MacroSheds v1.0 alpha, so all our major features are in place, but there might still be some feature bugs or data errors. Please let us know if you find any! Beta release will be early 2022.


Check out the Site Comparison tab to explore patterns across all sites within MacroSheds, or the Time Series page to closely compare time-series data for a few sites. When you're ready for analysis, head to the Data tab where you can download everything we've put together.

Dataset summary (see catalogues under the Data tab for more):

  • Time series of concentration and flux for dozens of solutes, as well as water quality metrics, at hundreds of stream/river monitoring sites.
  • Time series of discharge (flow) for all sites.
  • Time series of watershed-average precipitation volume, chemistry, and chemical flux for most sites.
  • Terrain, landcover, vegetation, geology, and climate summary statistics for the watersheds contributing to all sites.
  • Sample type (sensor measurement, grab sample, etc.) identified for all stream data points.
  • Uncertainty propagated through all MacroSheds stream data processing operations.
  • All temporally imputed and flagged data points marked for easy filtering.

Future updates:

  • Up to 15-minute sensor data (for now all series are aggregated to daily intervals).
  • MacroSheds R package.
  • API for querying/downloading data.
  • Rain/snow partitioning of precipitation data.
